schnitzel

Wiener Schnitzel is one of Austria’s most iconic national dishes—an elegant yet straightforward preparation of tender veal, lightly breaded and pan-fried to a crisp, golden perfection. Its origins trace back to 19th-century Vienna, where it became a symbol of refined Central European cuisine. The hallmark of an authentic Wiener Schnitzel is its light, airy breading that gently puffs during frying, creating a delicate crust that never overshadows the juicy veal inside. Traditionally served with fresh lemon wedges and classic sides like warm potato salad, parsley potatoes, or tart lingonberry preserves, this dish strikes a beautiful balance between rich, savory flavor and refreshing accompaniments. Whether you're preparing it for a holiday dinner or a weekend meal, Wiener Schnitzel brings a taste of old-world comfort and timeless culinary tradition to the table.

Ingredients

  • 4 veal cutlets (about 5 oz each), pounded to 1/4 inch thickness
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup plain breadcrumbs (preferably homemade or fine)
  • Vegetable oil or clarified butter, for frying (about 1/2 inch deep in pan)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Recipe

    < li>Place the veal cutlets between two sheets of plastic wrap and gently pound with a meat mallet until about 1/4 inch thick. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  1. Set up three shallow dishes: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with breadcrumbs.
  2. Dredge each cutlet in flour, shaking off the excess. Dip into the beaten eggs, then coat thoroughly with breadcrumbs. Do not press the crumbs into the meat—this helps the coating puff up while frying.
  3. Heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil or clarified butter in a large skillet over medium to medium-high heat until shimmering.
  4. Fry each cutlet one at a time (or two if your pan allows), for 2–3 minutes per side, until golden brown and crispy. Gently shake the pan while frying to encourage even browning and “soufflé” effect.
  5. Transfer the schnitzels to a paper towel–lined plate to drain excess oil. Keep warm in a low oven if needed.
  6. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and your choice of traditional sides like potato salad, parsley potatoes, or lingonberry jam.
