Bolognese

A classic Bolognese spaghetti is a rich and savory Italian dish that combines slow-cooked ground beef, pork, and aromatic vegetables like onions, carrots, and celery. Simmered in a flavorful mix of tomatoes, red wine, and a touch of cream, this hearty sauce is the epitome of comfort. The sauce is then gently tossed with al dente spaghetti, allowing the flavors to meld beautifully, and finished with a generous sprinkle of Parmesan cheese. It's a warm, satisfying meal that brings together the best of simple, rustic Italian cooking.

This Bolognese sauce recipe starts by gently sautéing a base of finely chopped onion, carrot, and celery in olive oil until tender. Garlic is added for extra depth, followed by a mix of ground beef and pork, which are browned until fully cooked. A splash of red wine deglazes the pan, enriching the flavor and lifting up any caramelized bits. Crushed tomatoes and tomato paste are then stirred in, along with dried oregano and basil, creating a rich tomato base.

The sauce is simmered slowly to allow the flavors to meld, then finished with milk or cream to add smoothness and a hint of sweetness. Seasoned with salt and pepper to taste, the result is a hearty, comforting sauce that pairs perfectly with pasta. This traditional method builds layers of flavor, making it a perfect choice for a cozy meal or dinner with guests.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/4 cup red wine
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup whole milk or heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery. Sauté for 5–7 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the ground beef and ground pork. Cook until browned, breaking up the meat with a spoon.
  5. Pour in the red wine to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits. Simmer for 3–4 minutes.
  6. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  7. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat and cook for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add the milk or cream and continue to simmer for another 10–15 minutes.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper, if needed.
  10. Serve the sauce over pasta and top with freshly grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.
